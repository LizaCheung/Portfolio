:CHOCOOLATE Mobile Browsing Experience

Mobile UX enhancement

Cohesive Browsing Experience

The luxury fashion brand :CHOCOOLATE is adapting their business model and seeking the opportunity to enter and leverage the digital marketplace.


As the mobile design lead, I was responsible for crafting a fluid, pleasurable mobile experience.

Identifying the problems

Fashion has a vital impact on self-satisfaction of the individual. Shopping for colorful clothes and shining accessories is a personal as well as an emotional experience. Shopping also keeps people updated on the classic, trendy style, and also it is the entry towards articulating their personality while socializing with family and friends.


Apprehend the touching emotions throughout the task flow and make the shoppers feel exciting will help the brand make long-lasting customer relationships.


Based on the research, combined with the requirements from our client and the BA team, I came up with my design objective: Create a non-distractive, immersive browsing experience.

Creating emotional experience

Content-centered immersive design

While viewing the product list, we want our users to immerse themselves in the products, ensuring that content is paramount.


I made this prototype to show how our users will experience the flow. On scrolling up, the sticky navigation scrolls off the screen, while the filter and sort toolbar is pinned at the most top. In doing so, only the most relative functions — filter and sort are at hand while viewing. Check out the nuances in the transition animation.

First launch - Chinese version

Filtering and sorting made easy

Always remember to layout the most relative functions at hand and make them easy to access. According to the metrics, filtering and sorting are the top two functionalities used on the list page.


The design embraces the two features and makes them accessible and easy to find. The filter and sort sticks to the top while scrolling, making them easy to discover and signify strong cue to tap. The challenge here is how to transition back and forth. A full-screen dialog or a half-screen overlay? The answer is in the design itself. :p

Transitions between different screens. See more in the Prototype.
