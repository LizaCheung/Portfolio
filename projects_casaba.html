Designing CASABA’s WeChat Mini Program Builder

CASABA is a powerful WeChat Mini Program builder that enables small-mid sized enterprises to run their business on the prevailing top No.1 social media in China: WeChat. With over 1 billion users on WeChat, it is much easier to boost high conversion rate. Our innovative e-Commerce tool is able to lead business to a new level. Unlike traditional e-Commerce tools, which are all about static data, endless lists and require tedious manual entry, CASABA’s more intelligent and automated platform enables each and every business to create e-shop on WeChat platform with only one click. Until now, more features are being introduced, evaluated and iterated…


It is simple, easy and automatic.

The Story

CASABA was born in 2018.


I joined the CASABA team at the very beginning, seeing the product grow from 0 to 1. My first year with CASABA is all about building the structure of the product from scratch, leveraging the established BAOZUN e-Commerce ecosystem and gaining a market advantage in response to the company’s high-level strategy.


The product obtained a 10x growth in the user base. We had ups and downs yet we quickly recovered via instantaneous iterations and delivered best solutions to our users. How did we make it? There’s a plethora of stories behind our endeavors. Here are three of the stories I’ll be sharing with you.

Design Objective

User Needs + Business Needs

“The one-click WeChat Mini Program generator enables people like me with little tech knowledge to open e-shop in an easier and faster way. Plus, the e-shop decorator helps me to customize the brand visual identity. It would be great if there’s an automated tool to create promotion campaigns that are popular out there in the market…”

User Voice + NPS Survey

This is a complex product and it takes more than a design team to achieve the goal. That’s why we looped in people from engineering, product, marketing and sales teams from day 1. In the 1-week workshops, we did a comprehensive analysis from our competitors, discussed with the product and marketing teams the research findings and insights, sorted out the priorities of the many features.

We analyzed BAOZUN persona data, did market research with the marketing and product teams. We conducted user interviews to gain insights and identify the real pain points.

Co-design Sessions + Workshops

We held workshops to share and discuss the research findings with our engineering and product teams in order to figure out the design direction.

We started with exploring how the 2B UI maps the 2C UI. To decrease the users’ cognitive load, we divided the UI into two panels. The preview panel on the left dynamically refreshes when the editing panel on the right is making changes.

Layout: Preview Panel & Editing Panel

We were thinking to make the editing action an in-place manipulation in the first place. Clicking the widgets on the left preview panel opens the respective editing panel on the right. And clicking the Preview button opens a new page to preview the frontend in the browser and also offers a QR-code for users to scan and view on the cell phones. This is even more straightforward and without second thought the users can easily map the CASABA to the WeChat Mini Program. However, due to some technical constraints we will leave it to the future iterations.

We tweaked the design of the product selector back and forth concerning the complexity of SPU and SKU. The user selects SPU (a product, on the left panel) first and then SKU (various attributes of this product, on the right panel). To help our users understand the hierarchy of the selection, we created this left-right layout. I’m glad this design is integrated into UNEX style guide.


We also added filter function on the title bar to help our users quickly select products among thousands of stock.


P.S. We later added some feasibility to the design:

Added a Drag icon between the two panels. It might block the view as the title becomes lengthy and the browser is scaled up.

Added a Refresh icon to the most left of the search field. As the stock status changes all the time, manual refresh helps to check stock status before error msg pops up.

Product Selector: SPU & SKU

To decrease confusion and frustration, we created new styles of states for the UI components to distinguish SPU selector from SKU selector.


Although our users can always reach out to Help Center to find answers, it’s important to give instant help in place. These handy tooltips offers clear explanations and guidance.

Clear Affordance & Tooltips

We cannot avoid errors, but we can help users with their errors. Of course we can always give all the information the users need such as the red error messages near the text fields or a popup warning, but do they always work for such a complex product? The answer is NO.


We may identify most of the error states but we cannot map them to meaningful sources because of a lack of context. A better approach is to set default values for the users ahead of time.

With only 6 months given, we adopted a mix of low-fi, mid-fi and high-fi design to ensure the engineering, design and product teams are in sync. In the initial stage, low-fi fast prototype is most effective in communicating design directions and explorations. Can you imagine that we relied on this low-fi prototype to decide on key points? The stories are in the details.

The Challenge

Our target users are mainly small to midsized business owners, to many of whom CROWDFUND is a new concept and even might be out of their knowledge. The question is how do we onboard new users and educate them?


How do we help users to set up their CROWDFUND rules and map them to WeChat Mini Program when some of the rules can be only controlled in CASABA?


With these questions in mind, we were dedicated to solve complex problems through user-centered design methodology.

A piece of the interaction specs

Design for First-Time Users

It takes time to fully understand the complications in CROWDFUND. We want to make sure our users learn fast and efficiently. We looped people in from the product documentation team and legal department to audit copy writing. Onboarding tips will only be triggered based on user actions and tooltips gives clear explanations of terminology. We also created a full page of guidance in Help Center.

False Mapping

In the campaign list page, we came up with the idea of giving full control to our users to set display priority of the undergoing campaigns in WeChat Mini Program in order to help the business to manage and operate their hot products in store. However, we quickly discovered that in this scenario simply a priority icon can signify false affordance that it’s used for this list page instead of WeChat Mini Program. There's no meaning of doing so in this page. While adding text, tooltip or directing to Help Center might help, it increases the learning curve…. We decided to leave this feature to future iterations.
