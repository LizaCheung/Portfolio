Citrix Recruitment System

Recruiting experience redesign

Citrix recruiting system is an internal tool for hiring staff to manage candidates. The current design is a universal solution for global offices. However, it might not be a good fit for our newly-built site…


As the lead design on this project, I was responsible for the responsive website design and the back-end management system UX enhancement.

Design Objectives


User Needs + Product Goals

Before I dived into design, I interviewed several users and collected some feedbacks. We also went through several rounds of brainstorming sessions. Analyzing from multiple aspects, I came up with my design objectives.


1. Responsive website for applicants vs. management system for recruiting staff

Responsive website:

In this initiative, we aimed to design a brand-new interface for our applicants — fresh-grads mainly, and make it responsive. In the first stage, we’ll launch the Chinese version. The English version will soon be released in the next iteration.

Management system:

For the management system, we would keep the general structure but cut off large portion of the functions and redesign the dashboard. We’ll focus on simplicity and customization. As a designer,  I need to show empathy for our engineers — consider time and technical constraints during design process.


2. Consistent style

Keep the look and feel aligned with global site.


3. Simplify flow, improve efficiency

What’s a great online recruiting experience? Our applicants abandon the form easily due to the complex form fields and server problem. Meanwhile, the old management system almost drove the hiring staff crazy. They need a more efficient tool to ease their daily work. 


4. Vision and flexibility

The future vision of the product will be more international and the user-base will be larger. I need to consider the product vision and flexibility into my design.

In terms of the look and feel, I adopted the global site. Each page was thoughtfully designed in regards of the information, text, images, video, color, and CTA button color and size. Into the management UI…

The management interface was quite complex and unintuitive. I offered some consultancy on the current design based on the following principles.



Design Principles:

1. Immediate feedback

The design should offer Immediate feedback right after user interacts with the interface.

2. Strong signifier

The design elements (signifier), e.g. icon, should be intuitive.

3. Affordance

The affordance of the design should be in consistent with user cognition.

4. Consistent style guide

Copy writing, visual elements, interaction methods (animations) etc. should be consistent.



In a nutshell, “Don’t make me think.”

Validate the design

We conducted two rounds of usability testings and the result came out well. Most of the designs have a 100% successful rate. The CAT buttons on key pages have higher click through rate after being redesigned. I was able to identify some of the usability issues and would be working on to iterate the design. Stay tuned for further iterations.
