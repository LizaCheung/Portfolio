Reebok Responsive Web Login Experience

More flexible and faster, with social network

Login and Register with One Click Using Social Network Account

To help our client Reebok grow and retain users, we aim to integrate social login with existing login/registration system.


As the Reebok mobile design lead, I’m responsible for the mobile login UX improvement.

People hate long signup form

Have you ever had the experience of giving up the long form page in the middle? According to our research, most people are bothered by having to create new accounts on websites. 90% of the users will leave a site instead of resetting or recovering login info. No user base, no engagement, and no conversion rates. Reebok needs more registered members.

A better registration solution

The iterations are based on previous research data and design critique with designers in the team. Based on our research, combined with the business requirements, I came up with the following design objectives:

1. User - quicker and smoother registration process


The site is under risk of losing users due to long signup form. A quicker and smoother registration/login process is required to increase sign-up rate.

2. User - engagement


User engagement is important for a site to succeed and continuously crease revenues. The new design allows users to link their existing accounts to a social network and encourages users to share website content to their social network accounts.

3. Business - higher conversion rates


The new registration/login experience aims to enlarge the user base, improve user engagement and increase conversion rates finally.  

4. Vision - boost higher-quality email marketing


The vision of the brand in the future is to enable the system to gather demographic data about target users, improve data quality and boost email marketing. I need to consider flexibility in my design.

Registered members may tie their account to a social network account now.

Now registered members who previously have tied their account to a social network could login by simply clicking on the social media icon.

Non-members may click on social login to fulfill registration and social bounding at the same time. It’s easy and fast, with no information overload.

How to organize and layout the information that would be visually pleasing, legible to the users? What is the right amount of information on the first screen of a mobile device that will not overload the users cognitively? The answers are in the design.

Validate the design solution

We conducted 2 rounds of usability testings. Most of the users can accomplish the task flow smoothly and quickly as expected. According to the metrics, the new design met the initial goals I set before I dived into the design. The bounce rate on the registration page is reduced in a large scale.

In the future iterations, there would be a lot more to dig into about how on earth the users behave in the stage of registration. How long do they stay on the page? What content of the page do they scan first and for how long do they retain? … The problems remain to be solved and more research methods like eye tracking and A/B test would be included.
